Autonomous agents & workflow intelligence.

The current frontier. Systems that take action: planning, using tools, and completing real work under human oversight.

The frontier.

An assistant that answers a question is useful. A system that can plan a multi-step task, call the tools and APIs to carry it out, and coordinate to finish real work is a different order of thing, and a harder one to make trustworthy.

The difficulty lies in reliability over long horizons, safe tool use, and the judgement to know when to hand back to a person.

We design for knowing when to act and when to ask. Nothing touches a real system until it has been measured against rigorous evaluations. Autonomy is earned against evidence.

What we build.

01End-to-end agentic systemsOrchestrating a multi-step process from start to finish: decomposing the goal, sequencing the work, and recovering when a step fails.
02Tool-using assistants in real softwareAgents that operate inside the environments work actually happens in, calling functions and APIs and reading and writing real state, under defined permissions.
03Multi-agent architectures under supervisionSpecialised agents that divide a problem and coordinate on it, with a supervising layer that keeps the system coherent and accountable.
04Human-in-the-loop control layersGuardrails, approval gates, and audit trails, so a person stays in command, able to see what was done and why, and to step in before anything irreversible.
05Adaptive workflow automationProcesses that adjust to the case in front of them, and escalate when they reach the edge of what they should decide alone.
